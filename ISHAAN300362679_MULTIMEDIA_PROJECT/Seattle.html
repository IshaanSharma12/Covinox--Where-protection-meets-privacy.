Bill Gates was born here, so after starting Microsoft he relocated it to the Seattle area. The company was originally started in Albuquerque, but they found it hard to recruit there and wanted to move to a larger city. Later, Jeff Bezos decided where to locate Amazon based on two factors: talent and taxes. He knew he could get great engineers from Microsoft. And he knew he would have to collect taxes from residents of the state he chose to locate in, so that led him to choose a smaller state like Washington instead of California or New York. It also has lower taxes in general.

Google started a facility in Seattle to attract Microsoft employees, then expanded it to attract Amazon employees. Cloud was and is a central focus. As the cloud business has exploded, Google's presence here has grown tremendously. Facebook is a slightly newer entry into the Seattle job market but is now targeting about 5k employees here. There are also offices for most of the other large tech companies.

Part of the reason Seattle is growing so much as a tech hub right now is that it's much cheaper than the Bay Area. There is no income tax and houses are half the price. For the most part people aren't moving from San Francisco to Seattle but rather new growth is being targeted for Seattle rather than the Bay. But also, Microsoft and Amazon are two of the largest companies in the world, often and so even with nothing else, Seattle would be a major tech city.
